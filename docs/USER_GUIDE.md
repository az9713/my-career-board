## What is my-career-board?

my-career-board (Personal Career Governance System) treats your career like a company - with you as the CEO reporting to a board of directors.

> **Inspired by Nate Jones:**
> - Video: ["Most People Want Validation, Not Perspective (Why This Matters Now)"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BaC5FEN2e4Y)
> - Article: ["My honest field notes on being my own unreliable narrator"](https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/the-rarest-thing-in-work-why-360)

Unlike a real board, these AI directors are:

- **Always available** - no scheduling needed
- **Brutally honest** - no politics or hidden agendas
- **Focused on you** - they know your specific challenges
- **Persistent** - they remember what you said and hold you accountable

### The Core Idea

Most career advice is generic. my-career-board is personalized to YOUR specific situation:

1. **You define your problems** - What are you actually paid to solve?
2. **AI directors challenge you** - Are you avoiding hard truths?
3. **You make commitments** - What will you do this quarter?
4. **The system tracks accountability** - Did you do what you said?

### Key Features

| Feature | Purpose | Time Required |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Problem Portfolio** | Define what you're paid to solve | 15-20 min (one-time) |
| **Quick Audit** | Fast accountability check | 15 min |
| **Board Meeting** | Deep quarterly review | 45-60 min |
| **Session History** | Review past discussions | 5 min |
| **Pattern Detection** | Spot recurring avoidance | Automatic |

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Create Your Account

1. Open the application in your web browser
2. Click **"Get Started"** on the landing page
3. Fill in your information:
   - **Full Name**: Your name (used in personalized greetings)
   - **Email**: Your email address (used for login)
   - **Password**: Choose a strong password
4. Click **"Create Account"**

### Step 2: Set Up Your Portfolio (Required)

Before you can use the board meeting feature, you need to define at least 3 problems you're paid to solve.

1. After signing up, you'll see a prompt to set up your portfolio
2. Click **"Set Up Portfolio"**
3. Follow the setup wizard (detailed instructions below)

### Step 3: Choose Your First Session

Once your portfolio is set up, you can:
- **Quick Audit**: 15-minute accountability check (recommended for first session)
- **Board Meeting**: Full quarterly review with all 5 directors

## Setting Up Your Problem Portfolio

Your Problem Portfolio is the foundation of my-career-board. It defines what you're actually paid to solve, which allows the AI directors to give you relevant, personalized feedback.

### What is a "Problem"?

A problem isn't a task or project - it's an ongoing challenge you're responsible for. Good examples:

| Good Problems | Why |
|--------------|-----|
| "Scaling our data pipeline to handle 10x growth" | Ongoing challenge with clear stakes |
| "Building a high-performing engineering team" | Continuous responsibility |
| "Reducing customer churn below 5%" | Measurable goal with ongoing effort |
| "Navigating the transition from IC to manager" | Career development challenge |

| Bad Problems | Why |
|--------------|-----|
| "Finish the Q1 report" | This is a task, not an ongoing problem |
| "Be a better employee" | Too vague to be actionable |
| "Meetings" | Not a problem you're solving |

### The Setup Process

**Step 1: Introduction**
Read the overview and click "Next"

**Step 2: Define Your Problems**

For each problem (minimum 3, maximum 5), provide:

1. **Problem Name** (required)
   - Short, descriptive title
   - Example: "AI-powered customer support"

2. **What Breaks If You Ignore This?** (required)
   - The consequences of neglecting this problem
   - Be specific about impact
   - Example: "Response times increase, customer satisfaction drops, support costs rise as we need more human agents"

3. **Time Allocation** (optional)
   - Percentage of your work time spent on this
   - All problems should add up to roughly 100%

**Step 3: Classify Each Problem**

For each problem, answer 3 questions:

1. **Is AI making this problem cheaper to solve?**
   - Yes = AI is helping
   - No = Still requires human expertise

2. **How costly is it when errors occur?**
   - High = Mistakes are very expensive (data loss, legal issues)
   - Low = Errors are easily corrected

3. **How much trust is required?**
   - High = Clients/stakeholders must trust you personally
   - Low = Results speak for themselves

Based on your answers, the system classifies each problem:

| Classification | Meaning |
|---------------|---------|
| **Appreciating** | Your skills are becoming MORE valuable. AI isn't replacing this. |
| **Depreciating** | AI is making this cheaper. Your skills may become less valuable. |
| **Stable** | Holding steady. Neither gaining nor losing value. |
| **Uncertain** | Can't determine yet. Watch this space. |

**Step 4: Review & Save**

Review all your problems and their classifications, then click "Save Portfolio".

### Editing Your Portfolio

You can always update your portfolio:
1. Go to Dashboard ‚Üí Problem Portfolio
2. Click "Add Problem" to add more
3. (Future feature: Edit existing problems)

## Running a Quick Audit

A Quick Audit is a 15-minute accountability check. It's less intensive than a full board meeting but keeps you honest between quarters.

### Starting a Quick Audit

1. From your Dashboard, click **"Start Audit"** on the blue Quick Audit card
2. The audit will begin immediately

### The Audit Process

You'll be asked a series of questions. For each question:

1. **Read the question carefully**
2. **Type your honest response**
3. **Be specific** - vague answers will be challenged

### Specificity Gates

The system includes "specificity gates" - if your answer is too vague, you'll be challenged to be more specific.

**Example:**

> **Question:** What's one thing you've been avoiding?
>
> **Vague answer:** "Having a difficult conversation"
>
> **Gate response:** "With who? About what? Be specific - who, what, when."

The gate helps you get past surface-level responses to the real issues.

### Audit Questions

Typical audit questions include:
- What's one thing you've been avoiding this week?
- What commitment did you make but not keep?
- What hard truth are you not acknowledging?
- What's the most important thing you should do but won't?
- If you got fired tomorrow, what would you wish you had done?

### Completing the Audit

After answering all questions:
1. Your responses are saved automatically
2. You can view the transcript in History
3. Patterns from this audit contribute to pattern detection

## Conducting a Board Meeting

A Board Meeting is a comprehensive quarterly review with all 5 AI directors. It takes 45-60 minutes but provides deep accountability.

### Prerequisites

Before starting a board meeting:
- ‚úÖ You must have 3+ problems in your portfolio
- ‚úÖ Set aside 45-60 minutes of uninterrupted time
- ‚úÖ Have your notes from last quarter ready (if applicable)

### Starting a Board Meeting

1. From your Dashboard, click **"Enter Board Room"**
2. Select the quarter you're reviewing (e.g., "Q1 2025")
3. Click **"Start Meeting"**

### The Meeting Flow

The meeting has 6 phases, each led by a different director:

#### Phase 1: Opening (The Strategist)
*Sets context for the meeting*

> "Let's start with the big picture. What quarter are we reviewing, and what did you set out to accomplish?"

**What to share:**
- The time period you're reviewing
- Your main goals for that period
- Any major changes or events

#### Phase 2: Last Quarter Review (Accountability Hawk)
*Reviews commitments and results*

> "What were your specific bets from last quarter? Let's see the receipts."

**What to share:**
- Specific commitments you made
- What actually happened
- Evidence of success or failure

#### Phase 3: Avoidance Audit (Avoidance Hunter)
*Surfaces avoided decisions and conversations*

> "What decision have you been avoiding? Be specific."

**What to share:**
- Decisions you've postponed
- Conversations you haven't had
- Why you've been avoiding them

#### Phase 4: Market Check (Market Reality Skeptic)
*Assesses your market position*

> "How has your market value changed this quarter? What evidence do you have?"

**What to share:**
- Changes in your skills or experience
- Market trends affecting your role
- Concrete evidence of your value

#### Phase 5: Strategy Review (The Strategist)
*Evaluates long-term trajectory*

> "Zoom out: where is your current path leading in 5 years?"

**What to share:**
- Your long-term career direction
- Whether current work aligns with that
- Alternative paths you're considering

#### Phase 6: Next Quarter Bets (Devil's Advocate)
*Sets falsifiable commitments*

> "What are your bets for next quarter? Make them falsifiable."

**What to share:**
- Specific, measurable commitments
- How you'll know if you succeeded
- What would make these bets fail

### Director Interjections

Any director can interject during the meeting if something triggers their attention:

- **Accountability Hawk** interjects on broken promises
- **Market Reality Skeptic** interjects on unrealistic assessments
- **Avoidance Hunter** interjects on vague or evasive answers
- **The Strategist** interjects on short-term thinking
- **Devil's Advocate** interjects on weak logic

### Completing the Meeting

1. After all phases, the meeting ends automatically
2. Your full transcript is saved to History
3. Patterns are analyzed and added to your insights

## Reviewing Your History

All your sessions (audits and board meetings) are saved for future reference.

### Accessing History

1. From your Dashboard, click **"View all"** next to Recent Activity
   OR
   Click a specific session to view it

The History page shows all your sessions:
- **Type**: Quick Audit (blue) or Board Meeting (green)
- **Date**: When the session occurred
- **Messages**: Number of exchanges
- **Status**: Completed or In Progress

Click any session to see:
- **Header**: Session type, date, duration

- **Before board meetings**: Review your last meeting's commitments
- **Spot patterns**: See what topics keep coming up
- **Track progress**: Compare your answers over time

## Tips for Effective Sessions

### Before the Session

1. **Set aside dedicated time**
   - Quick Audit: 15 uninterrupted minutes
   - Board Meeting: 45-60 uninterrupted minutes

2. **Review your last session**
   - What did you commit to?
   - What actually happened?

3. **Gather evidence**
   - Metrics and data
   - Specific examples
   - Feedback you've received

4. **Prepare mentally**
   - Be ready for uncomfortable questions
   - Commit to honesty

### During the Session

1. **Be specific**
   - Names, dates, numbers
   - "I talked to Sarah last Tuesday" not "I had a conversation"

2. **Don't defend, describe**
   - State facts, not excuses
   - "I didn't do it" not "I couldn't do it because..."

3. **Welcome discomfort**
   - If you're uncomfortable, you're probably hitting truth
   - Lean into it

4. **Make falsifiable commitments**
   - "I will have the compensation conversation with my manager by January 15"
   - Not "I'll try to talk about compensation sometime"

### After the Session

1. **Review the transcript**
   - Capture commitments
   - Note patterns

2. **Schedule follow-up**
   - Put commitments on your calendar
   - Set reminders

3. **Act immediately**
   - Do one thing from the session today
   - Momentum matters

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Is my data private?**
A: Yes. Your data is stored locally on the server running the application. It is not shared with anyone.

**Q: How often should I use my-career-board?**
A: Recommended schedule:
- Quick Audit: Weekly or bi-weekly
- Board Meeting: Once per quarter

**Q: Can I delete my data?**
A: Currently, you can view your history but bulk deletion is not yet implemented. This feature is planned for a future update.

### Portfolio Questions

**Q: How many problems should I have?**
A: 3-5 problems is ideal. More than 5 becomes difficult to track, fewer than 3 might not capture your full scope.

**Q: What if my problems change?**
A: Update your portfolio! Career problems evolve. Review and update quarterly.

**Q: What if I'm unsure about classification?**
A: Choose "Uncertain" - this is honest. The system will help you clarify over time.

### Session Questions

**Q: What if I need to stop mid-session?**
A: Your progress is saved. Return to complete the session from where you left off.

**Q: Why are the directors so direct?**
A: By design. A polite board wouldn't serve you. The directness helps cut through self-deception.

**Q: Can I choose which director responds?**
A: No. The meeting phase determines the lead director. Others may interject based on your responses.

### Technical Questions

**Q: The page isn't loading. What do I do?**
A:
1. Refresh the page
2. Clear your browser cache
3. Make sure the server is running

**Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?**
A: Password reset is not yet implemented. Contact your system administrator.

**Q: The AI isn't responding.**
A:
1. Check your internet connection
2. The AI API might be overloaded - wait and try again
3. Check that the ANTHROPIC_API_KEY is configured

## Getting the Most Value

my-career-board works best when you:

1. **Use it consistently** - Sporadic use provides sporadic value
2. **Be brutally honest** - The AI can't help if you're not truthful
3. **Embrace discomfort** - Growth happens at the edge of comfort
4. **Act on insights** - Knowledge without action is useless
5. **Track progress** - Review history to see patterns and growth

Remember: my-career-board is a tool for self-accountability. It won't do the hard work for you - but it will make sure you can't hide from what you know you need to do.
